About Me

Welcome to my homepage!

I am Sayan Nandi, an MSc Chemistry candidate at Savitribai Phule Pune University, researching metal-organic frameworks (MOFs), catalyst design, and materials characterisation. I have hands-on experience synthesising and characterising MOFs and inorganic composite materials, including catalyst systems that achieved 100% selectivity in CO2-to-methanol conversion during a research exchange at the University of Stavanger, Norway.

My work spans spectroscopic, electrochemical, and microscopy techniques — FT-IR, XRD, XPS, SEM, EIS — for structure-property analysis of composite and catalytic materials.

My research interests span over,

  • Metal-organic frameworks (MOFs)
  • Heterogeneous catalysis
  • Materials characterisation & spectroscopy

I completed my B.Sc. (Honours) in Chemistry at New Alipore College, University of Calcutta (class of 2023).

Besides research, I enjoy tutoring and mentoring younger students, travelling, and following developments in sustainable chemistry. I'm driven to pursue research addressing real-world sustainability challenges, with a long-term goal of building an academic career that combines impactful research with training and mentoring the next generation of scientists.

Research Experience

Master's Thesis Research Project — 2025–2026
Supervisor: Prof. Nirmalya Ballav, Chair, Department of Chemistry, IISER Pune
  • Identified that in-situ reduction of graphene oxide during Cu-PBA synthesis drives interfacial charge transfer between composite layers, enhancing I-V response and charge storage capacity relative to pristine Cu-PBA and physically mixed controls.
  • Confirmed through spectroscopic (FT-IR, Raman, XPS, EPR, UV-Vis), microscopic (SEM), and electrical/electrochemical (I-V, Hall, CV, GCD, EIS) characterisation.
Student Exchange Research Programme — 2025
University of Stavanger, Norway — Supervisors: Dr. Sachin Chavan, Dr. Zhixin Yu
  • Achieved 100% selectivity for CO2 hydrogenation to methanol using a MOF-based catalyst system.
  • Synthesised uniformly distributed 5 nm copper nanoparticles with no detectable oxidation or sintering — addressing two persistent stability challenges in heterogeneous catalyst design.
  • Synthesised MOFs (Ce UiO-66, Ce UiO-66 bpy) and Cu/CuZn nanoparticles for catalytic applications, characterised via IR, NMR, XRD, TGA, BET, and SEM.
Bachelor's Dissertation Project — 2023
University of Calcutta — Supervisor: Dr. Rupa Mukhopadhyay
  • Conducted a literature review on sustainable seawater desalination and purification techniques, focusing on reverse osmosis (RO) and membrane-based separation processes.
  • Evaluated enhancement strategies for membrane efficiency and brine valorisation as approaches to addressing global freshwater scarcity.
