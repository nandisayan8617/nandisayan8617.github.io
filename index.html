About

I'm a chemistry researcher with hands-on experience synthesising and characterising metal-organic frameworks and inorganic composite materials — including catalyst systems that achieved 100% selectivity in CO2-to-methanol conversion during a research exchange in Norway.

My work spans spectroscopic, electrochemical, and microscopy techniques — FT-IR, XRD, XPS, SEM, EIS — for structure-property analysis of composite and catalytic materials. I'm driven to pursue research that addresses real-world sustainability challenges, and I'm building toward an academic career that combines impactful research with mentoring the next generation of scientists.

Research & Teaching Experience

2025 — 2026
Master's Thesis Research
Supervisor: Prof. Nirmalya Ballav, Chair, Dept. of Chemistry, IISER Pune
  • Identified that in-situ reduction of graphene oxide during Cu-PBA synthesis drives interfacial charge transfer between composite layers, enhancing I-V response and charge storage capacity relative to pristine Cu-PBA and physically mixed controls.
  • Confirmed findings via spectroscopic (FT-IR, Raman, XPS, EPR, UV-Vis), microscopic (SEM), and electrical/electrochemical (I-V, Hall, CV, GCD, EIS) characterisation.
2025
Student Exchange Research Programme
University of Stavanger, Norway — Supervisors: Dr. Sachin Chavan, Dr. Zhixin Yu
  • Achieved 100% selectivity for CO2 hydrogenation to methanol using a MOF-based catalyst system.
  • Synthesised uniformly distributed 5 nm copper nanoparticles with no detectable oxidation or sintering, addressing two persistent stability challenges in heterogeneous catalyst design.
  • Synthesised MOFs (Ce UiO-66, Ce UiO-66 bpy) and Cu/CuZn nanoparticles for catalytic applications, characterised via IR, NMR, XRD, TGA, BET, and SEM.
